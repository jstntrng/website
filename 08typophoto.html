Typophoto

Compositions that seamlessly mesh type and image. I was given three words from a passage in Robert Bringhurst's "The Elements of Typographic Style."

The passage reads:
“...by all means leave the road when you wish. That is precisely the use of a road: to reach individually chosen points of departure. By all means break the rules, and break them beautifully, deliberately, and well. This is one of the ends for which they exist.”
